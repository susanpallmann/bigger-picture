Wild Population

Key Threat

Poaching

The northern white rhinoceros is a distinct subspecies of the white rhinoceros, once found across central Africa. Poaching in the '70s and '80s led to population declines until it had reached a low of just 15 individuals. Conservation efforts over the next two decades allowed the population to double, but this would not last. As poaching intensified once more, the northern white rhinoceros went extinct in the wild by 2008. At the beginning of this year, there were just three individuals living semi-wild at the Ol Pejeta Conservancy in Kenya. By March, the last male had died. San Diego Zoo is now looking into using modern genetic technology to implant a northern white rhinoceros embryo into a female of the closely-related southern white rhinoceros. If this is successful, there is hope that northern white rhinoceroses might roam free once more.
