Key Threat

Deforestation

The golden lion tamarin is a small, colourful monkey found in the Atlantic Forest of South America. This area of rainforest has been subject to extensive logging in the past, leading to the destruction of the tamarin's home. By the 1970s, only a handful of pockets of suitable habitat remained for the species. Its population dropped to a low of just a few hundred and it seemed that the golden lion tamarin was headed for extinction. However, they have made a comeback through the the efforts of the "Golden Lion Tamarin Conservation Program", run by the National Zoo in Washington, DC. Deforested areas are being bought back and restored, with wild populations supported through the release of captive-bred individuals. By 2000, about 350 tamarins had been released into the wild from zoos. Now, at least one third of the wild golden lion tamarin population are descended from captive-bred individuals.
