Key Threat

Poaching

The addax is a stunning antelope of northern Africa with extravagant, twisting horns. Unfortunately, these horns are valuable in the black market, and poaching has led to rapid population declines. Political instability and the search for oil have made conservation efforts in this region difficult, and allowed further poaching to go unrestricted. Addax now persist in only six wild populations, three of which are made up of individuals reintroduced from captivity. The total wild population is now probably less than 100 individuals, but a couple thousand survive in zoos and in captively-managed herds. Until the serious threats of poaching can be managed, maintaining a healthy population of addax in zoos is our best chance to ensure the species can remain for years to come.
