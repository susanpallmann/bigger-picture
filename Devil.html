Key Threat

Disease

In 1996, the Devil Facial Tumour Disease (DFTD) was discovered. This was a contagious cancer known only to Tasmanian devils, with symptoms including the formation of large tumours on the face. The disease is always lethal, killing infected animals within about 6 months. However, within that time, infected devils can spread the disease to other individuals through biting, a natural display of aggression. As a result, the disease has spread across most of Tasmania in the last 20 years, wiping out 85% of the population. To combat this, zoos in Australia, Europe and the United States are involved in holding and maintaining healthy captive populations. Now, there are more than 700 captive devils held in about 50 zoos around the world. Some of these are being successfully released onto Maria Island to join a wild, disease-free population.
